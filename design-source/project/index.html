№ 01 · Principles

How a pen meets its paper.

Five axes the editors weigh when proposing a marriage.
i.

Wetness × Absorbency

A generous flow demands a paper that sips, not gulps. Match a wet nib to sized stock; a dry feed forgives almost anything.

DrySopping
ii.

Nib Size × Tooth

Finer nibs reward a polished surface; broader nibs come alive on the slightest tooth, where the line earns its texture.

GlassToothy
iii.

Sheen × Smoothness

Sheen — the ink's second colour — wants pooling, and pooling wants a glassy, low-absorbency sheet. Cotton will refuse it.

MatteMirror
iv.

Flex × Sizing

A flex nib lays down a startling line; without sizing the railroad becomes a feathered field. Pair vintage with vintage.

ModernHeritage
v.

Use × Mood

A journal at dawn is not a letter at night. The right pairing makes the act of writing match its hour.

PAIRING № 047 · EDITOR'S CHOICE

Custom 823 ×
Tomoe River

A wet vacuum-filler finds its translucent altar.

The 823's medium 14k delivers ink in such generous helpings that most papers buckle or bleed. Tomoe River, paradoxically thin at 52gsm, is one of the rare sheets that takes the flood without complaint — and rewards it with sheen so pronounced it changes colour under the desk lamp.

Reserve for letters and long-form journals. Allow forty seconds to dry.

PAIRING № 052 · CRISP

Lamy 2000 ×
Rhodia № 16

A modern fine on the most modern paper.

The Lamy's hooded 14k lays a controlled line — dry, neat, unsentimental — that finds its temperament on Rhodia's brilliant, near-glass surface. Sheen flashes briefly; bleed is none. The dot grid keeps an architect's hand honest.

For meeting notes that you might reread.

PAIRING № 061 · ROMANTIC

Nakaya Portable ×
Crown Mill Cotton

Urushi on laid cotton — the room hushes.

A soft-fine Nakaya gives the gentlest line variation; on Crown Mill's pure cotton laid, the ink settles into the chain-lines rather than running across them. Sheen quiets, shading flowers. This is paper for sealing wax.

For letters one writes in single sittings, by lamplight.

PAIRING № 074 · WORKMANLIKE

Pelikan M800 ×
Apica CD Premium

A broad German nib, kept in line by a quiet Japanese ruled.

The M800's broad 18k tends to swagger; Apica's sized, 81gsm ivory makes it write a single steady line, ruling to ruling. Sheen is muted; shading is honest. A daily-driver pairing for the desk.

For ledgers, plans, and the longer notebook entries.
